Transforming Water Management in Udachiwadi Village

Udachiwadi, a village in Maharashtra, faced severe water scarcity, impacting agriculture and livelihoods. To combat this, the community, under the leadership of Shri Vilasrao Salunkhe and coordinated locally by dedicated farmer Yogesh Magar, established the Pani Panchayat. This initiative focused on equitable water distribution and sustainable resource management.


  • Water Conservation Structures:

    Construction of farm ponds, continuous contour trenches (CCTs), and percolation pits to capture rainwater, enhance groundwater recharge, and prevent soil erosion.


  • Efficient Irrigation:

    Implementation of rotational and drip irrigation systems to optimize water use, ensuring reliable year-round agriculture.


  • Community Governance:

    Open membership to all farmers, with decisions made by consensus to maintain transparency and fairness in water allocation.

    • These measures resulted in improved water availability, increased crop yields, and reduced seasonal migration, as farmers gained stable access to water. Despite challenges like infrastructure maintenance and funding constraints, solutions included leveraging government schemes such as MGNREGA and fostering community involvement. Future plans aim to scale water conservation efforts, expand infrastructure, promote crop diversification, and advance training in sustainable practices.
