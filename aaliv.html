Aaliv (Lepidium sativum) is typically ready for harvest about 2 to 3 weeks after planting. You can start harvesting the leaves once they reach around 4 to 6 inches in height. For the best flavor, it's best to harvest them before they start to flower. Simply snip the leaves off just above the soil line, and they can continue to grow for a second harvest if conditions are right.

  • Planting

Aaliv seeds grow best when they are sown directly into the garden., and the crop matures in 30-40 days. In Udachiwadi, Aaliv is grown during both the Kharif and Rabi seasons.

  • Irrigation

Aaliv requires minimal water, and in many cases, farmers in Udachiwadi rely solely on rain-fed irrigation or minimal water from farm ponds.

  • Challenges

The short growing cycle of Aaliv means that farmers need to be cautious of sudden weather changes, which can affect the crop yield.
