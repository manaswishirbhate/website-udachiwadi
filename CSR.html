Cybage

CybageAsha, the CSR arm of Cybage, has been a key player in rural upliftment, with a strong focus on education, healthcare, and infrastructure development. CybageAsha’s initiatives aim to improve the quality of life in Udachiwadi by addressing fundamental needs and enabling long-term growth.

Healthcare

  • ● Medical Camps:

    CybageAsha regularly organizes medical camps that provide free health check-ups, consultations, and treatments.

  • ● Awareness Programs:

    In addition to medical services, CybageAsha conducts workshops and awareness campaigns on hygiene, nutrition, and preventive healthcare. These programs aim to educate the villagers about basic health practices.

Education

  • ● School Infrastructure:

    CybageAsha has renovated classrooms, built libraries, and improved sanitation facilities to ensure better learning environments.

  • ●Scholarships:

    Financial aid is provided to underprivileged, high-achieving students, encouraging further education.

  • ● Digital Learning:

    Introduction of computer labs and digital literacy programs ensures students are equipped with modern skills.

Infrastructure Development

  • ●Sanitation:

    Cybage has actively worked on improving sanitation facilities by constructing toilets. This effort is complemented by campaigns promoting clean sanitation practices, aimed at reducing open defecation and encouraging proper waste management.

  • ● Water Conservation:

    CybageAsha has undertaken water conservation initiatives such as rainwater harvesting and the construction of check dams. These projects ensure that the village has access to a sustainable water supply, particularly during dry seasons.

CybageAsha has made impactful strides in Udachiwadi, a drought-prone village in Purandar taluka, Pune. In 2019, the organization inaugurated various village development projects aimed at improving infrastructure and enhancing the overall quality of life for residents. One of the standout initiatives was a successful tree plantation drive held in 2023, which not only focused on environmental sustainability but also aimed to mitigate the effects of soil erosion and enhance the village's green cover. Additionally, CybageAsha installed soak pits throughout the village to effectively manage rainwater and recharge groundwater, addressing the critical issue of water scarcity. Together, these efforts contribute significantly to the sustainable development and resilience of Udachiwadi, helping the community thrive despite environmental challenges.
